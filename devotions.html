The Presence of the Lord

June 15, 2026 • Devotion

⏱ 4 Minute Read

Life moves quickly. Responsibilities multiply, unexpected challenges arise, and distractions compete for our attention. Yet through every season, one truth remains unchanged: the greatest place we can be is in the presence of the Lord. His presence is not reserved for a select few or limited to a church service. Every believer has the invitation to draw near to Him and experience His peace, strength, and unfailing love.

David declared:

“You will show me the path of life; In Your presence is fullness of joy; At Your right hand are pleasures forevermore.”

Psalm 16:11 (NKJV)

(Continue)

The joy found in God's presence is deeper than happiness based on circumstances. It remains steady when life is uncertain and becomes a source of strength when our hearts grow weary. His presence satisfies the deepest needs of our souls in a way that nothing else can.

Moses understood this truth when leading Israel through the wilderness. More than a promised land or earthly success, he desired the Lord to remain with His people.

“If Your Presence does not go with us, do not bring us up from here.”

Exodus 33:15 (NKJV)

Today, God extends the same invitation to us. Through prayer, worship, and meditation on His Word, we can enjoy continual fellowship with Him. No valley is too dark and no burden is too heavy for the One who walks beside His children.

His promise still stands:

“I will never leave you nor forsake you.”

Hebrews 13:5 (NKJV)

Whatever lies before you today, remember that the Lord is near. His presence brings peace to anxious hearts, wisdom for difficult decisions, strength for weary souls, and hope that reaches beyond every trial. The more we seek Him, the more we discover that His presence is not simply a place to visit, but a place to live every day.

Prayer

Heavenly Father, thank You for the gift of Your presence. Help me seek You above everything else and find my joy, peace, and strength in You alone. Draw me closer to Your heart and teach me to walk with You throughout every moment of my day. Let Your presence guide my steps, sustain my faith, and fill my life with lasting hope. In Jesus' name, Amen.

Verse

“Draw near to God and He will draw near to you.”

James 4:8 (NKJV)

What a God!

May 18, 2026 • Devotion

⏱ 6 Minute Read

There are times in life when I sit back and just shake my head in amazement at how faithful God has been to me. Through every high and every low, every answered prayer and every season where I had to wait, God has never once failed me. When I didn’t know how things would work out, He already had a way made. When I felt weak, He carried me. When I lost hope, He reminded me that He was still in control. What a God!

Sometimes we think God’s faithfulness depends on our perfection, but I’m thankful it doesn’t. If that were the case, none of us would make it. His faithfulness comes from who He is, not from who we are. Even when I’ve fallen short, God has remained steady, loving, merciful, and true to His word.

The Bible says in Lamentations 3:22-23 (KJV):

“It is of the LORD'S mercies that we are not consumed, because his compassions fail not. They are new every morning: great is thy faithfulness.”

(Continue)

Every single day we wake up is proof that God is still being good to us. We may not have everything we want, but the fact that He keeps us, protects us, and gives us another chance is enough to thank Him for.

I’ve learned that God may not always come when I think He should, but He is never late. Looking back over my life, I can see His hand in situations that could have destroyed me. Doors opened that no man could shut. Peace came in moments that should have broken me. Somehow, through everything, God kept me standing.

Deuteronomy 7:9 (KJV) says:

“Know therefore that the LORD thy God, he is God, the faithful God, which keepeth covenant and mercy with them that love him and keep his commandments to a thousand generations.”

That scripture reminds me that God does not break His promises. If He said it, He will do it.

There are people who gave up on me, situations that didn’t go my way, and moments where I didn’t understand what God was doing. But now I can say that even in the confusion, He was working things out for my good. What looked like a setback was really God protecting me. What felt like delay was really preparation.

One thing about God. He knows how to take care of His people. He knows how to provide when resources look low. He knows how to comfort a hurting heart. He knows how to restore what life tried to take away.

Psalms 37:25 (KJV) says:

“I have been young, and now am old; yet have I not seen the righteous forsaken, nor his seed begging bread.”

That verse is a reminder that God does not abandon His children. His track record is too consistent for me to doubt Him now.

When I think about His grace, His patience, His protection, and His unconditional love, all I can say is: What a God! Nobody else could have kept me the way He has. Nobody else could love us despite our flaws and still continue to call us His own.

So no matter what season you may be in right now, keep trusting Him. Keep praying. Keep believing. God is still faithful, and He is still working behind the scenes even when you cannot see Him moving.

One day you’ll look back over everything you survived and say the same thing:

What a God!

Preparing the Atmosphere for Revival

May 18, 2026 • Devotion

⏱ 7 Minute Read

Revival does not begin with crowds, noise, or excitement. Revival begins in hearts that have been prepared for the presence of God. Before the fire fell in the upper room, before the gospel spread through the nations, Jesus first taught His followers how to prepare the atmosphere spiritually.

In John 14, Jesus spoke peace into troubled hearts:

“Let not your heart be troubled: ye believe in God, believe also in me.”
John 14:1

Fear, anxiety, and unbelief create resistance to what God desires to do. Jesus understood that if the disciples were going to receive the promise of the Father, their hearts first needed to be anchored in faith and peace. Revival cannot flourish in an atmosphere ruled by panic and distraction. Faith prepares the atmosphere for the move of God.

(Continue)

Jesus then promised another Comforter, the Holy Ghost:

“He dwelleth with you, and shall be in you.”
John 14:17

The atmosphere for revival is prepared when people become aware of God’s presence and make room for the Holy Spirit to dwell freely among them. Revival is not manufactured by human ability; it is hosted by yielded hearts.

This truth is beautifully illustrated in the house of Martha and Mary. Martha was consumed with activity, but Mary positioned herself at Jesus’ feet.

“Mary hath chosen that good part…”
Luke 10:42

Martha had preparation in the natural sense, but Mary understood spiritual preparation. She created an atmosphere where the voice of Jesus became the center of attention. Revival often tarries where distraction dominates, but it flows where hunger for God becomes greater than the demands of life.

Many want revival power, but few want revival posture.

Mary teaches us that preparation for revival requires:

Stillness before God
Attentiveness to His voice
Surrender of distractions
Desire for His presence above all else

Then in Luke 24:49, Jesus gave one final instruction before Pentecost:

“Tarry ye in the city of Jerusalem, until ye be endued with power from on high.”

The disciples were not told to organize, strategize, or promote. They were told to wait. Waiting together in prayer and expectation prepared the atmosphere for the outpouring of the Holy Ghost.

Before Pentecost came suddenly, there was a season of tarrying faithfully.

The atmosphere for revival is prepared when believers:

Pray persistently
Gather in unity
Wait with expectation
Remove distractions
Trust the promises of God
Welcome the work of the Holy Spirit

Revival is not merely an event; it is the response of God to hearts that are ready for Him.

Today, God still seeks people who will prepare room for His presence. Hearts free from fear. Minds attentive to His Word. Lives surrendered in obedience. Churches committed to prayer. Believers willing to tarry until heaven moves.

If we desire revival in our homes, churches, and communities, the preparation must begin within us. The atmosphere changes when Jesus becomes the center again.

Prayer

Heavenly Father, prepare my heart for revival. Remove fear, distraction, and spiritual complacency from my life. Teach me to sit at Your feet like Mary and to wait upon You with expectation. Let my heart become a dwelling place for Your Spirit. Fill my home, my church, and my community with an atmosphere that welcomes Your presence and power. Revive me first, Lord, and let Your Spirit move freely in every area of my life. In Jesus’ name, Amen.

⏱ 4 Minute Read

There are moments when everything around me feels uncertain—when life shifts, people change, and even my own strength seems unreliable. But when I come back to the Word, I’m reminded of something steady, something unshakable: God has never changed, and He never will.

“I am Alpha and Omega, the beginning and the ending…” That truth settles me. Before anything in my life began, God already was. And long after my worries fade, He will still be God. He stands outside of time, yet He steps into my life with purpose and authority. There is nothing I will face that He hasn’t already seen. (Revelation 1:8)

(Continue)

When I read, “I am the first, and I am the last; and beside me there is no God,” I’m reminded that I don’t need to look anywhere else for security or direction. So often I’m tempted to rely on what I can see or control, but God alone holds everything together. He isn’t one option among many—He is the only true foundation. (Isaiah 44:6)

“Jesus Christ the same yesterday, and to day, and for ever.” That speaks directly to my fears. People can be inconsistent. Circumstances can shift overnight. But Jesus remains the same. The compassion He showed, the power He carried, the promises He made—they are still true right now. I don’t have to wonder who He will be for me today. He is faithful, just as He always has been. (Hebrews 13:8)

And in the middle of everything, “God is our refuge and strength, a very present help in trouble.” Not distant. Not delayed. Present. That means when I feel overwhelmed, I’m not facing it alone. He is right here, ready to be my shelter and my strength when mine runs out. (Psalm 46:1)

“Thy faithfulness is unto all generations…” That reminds me that what God has been for others, He will be for me. His track record is perfect. Every generation has seen His hand, and mine will too. (Psalm 119:90)

So today, I choose to rest in who God is—not just what He can do. He is the beginning and the end, my refuge, my constant, and my faithful God. And that is more than enough.

No End in Sight

May 2, 2026 • Devotion

⏱ 8 Minute Read

There’s something powerful about the way the Gospels describe Jesus—not as someone whose work had limits, but as someone whose works kept pointing beyond themselves. In John 2:11, His first miracle “manifested forth his glory; and his disciples believed on him.” It was just the beginning, but even then, it hinted at something much bigger.

By the time we reach John 20:30–31, we’re told plainly that not everything Jesus did could even be recorded. What is written is enough to lead us to belief—but it also suggests there’s more, always more, to who He is. His story doesn’t feel finished because His impact isn’t finished.

(Continue)

When the winds and waves obey Him in Matthew 8:27, the disciples ask, “What manner of man is this?” That question still echoes. The more we encounter Christ, the more we realize we haven’t reached the end of understanding Him yet.

And even after His resurrection, the work continues. Mark 16:20 says the disciples went forth preaching, “the Lord working with them.” Jesus didn’t step away—He remained active, present, moving through His people.

So what does that mean for us?

It means that whatever we’re facing, whatever feels unfinished or uncertain, God’s work in our lives isn’t at a stopping point. There is no “end” to His grace, no final chapter where He stops moving. Instead, He invites us in Matthew 11:28–30: “Come unto me… and I will give you rest.” Not rest because everything is over—but rest because He is still in control.

Reflection

Sometimes we feel like we’ve hit a wall—emotionally, spiritually, or physically. But the same Jesus who turned water into wine, calmed storms, and continued working through His disciples is still at work today. His story in your life isn’t finished.

Prayer

Lord, thank You that Your work never ends. When I feel stuck or weary, remind me that You are still moving. Help me trust You, even when I can’t see the full picture. Give me rest in Your presence and faith to keep walking forward. Amen.

He’s Still in the Boat

⏱ 4 Minute Read

There are seasons where life just feels like too much all at once. You’re doing your best to follow God, trying to stay faithful, but everything around you feels unsettled—like a storm you didn’t ask for.

In Matthew 8:23–27, the disciples were right where they were supposed to be—following Jesus—and still found themselves in the middle of a violent storm. That always stands out to me.

Being in God’s will doesn’t mean life will be calm. Sometimes it means trusting Him when everything around you says you shouldn’t be at peace.

(Continue)

The part that gets me is that Jesus was in the boat the whole time. They weren’t alone, even though it felt like it.

Then I think about what Paul says in 2 Corinthians 11:27, He talks about being worn out, sleepless, hungry—just pushed to the limit. That’s real life. Following Christ doesn’t mean we won’t feel exhausted or stretched thin. It means we keep going, even there.

But Paul doesn’t stop there. In Philippians 4:11, he says he learned how to be content no matter what situation he was in. Learned—that means it didn’t come naturally. It came through walking with God in both the highs and the lows.

Contentment isn’t about everything going right; it’s about knowing God is still right there when things go wrong.

And the reason that kind of life is even possible comes down to Galatians 2:20 “It is no longer I who live, but Christ lives in me.” That changes everything.

My strength isn’t just my own. My peace isn’t just based on my circumstances. My life is rooted in Him now.

So when the storm hits, when I’m tired, when things don’t make sense—I come back to this: Jesus is still in the boat. He hasn’t left. And if He’s there, then I can keep trusting, even when the waves are loud.

At dawn, the women came to the tomb carrying spices, prepared to honor what they believed was the end. Their hopes had been buried alongside Jesus. Grief had settled in. Finality felt certain.

But the stone was rolled away.

Luke tells us that as they stood there, confused and afraid, two men in dazzling clothes spoke words that still echo through history: “Why do you seek the living among the dead? He is not here, but has risen.”

This question gently confronts not just the women—but us.

(Continue)

How often do we look for life in places marked by death? We search for peace in anxiety, purpose in achievement, identity in approval. Like the women at the tomb, we sometimes carry spices to situations God has already transformed—expecting endings where God has declared new beginnings.

The resurrection of Jesus is not just an event to remember; it is a reality to live in. It means that despair does not have the final word. It means that what seems sealed, lost, or broken is not beyond God’s power to restore.

The angels remind the women to remember what Jesus had said—that He would be crucified and rise again. And that remembering changes everything. Their sorrow turns into urgency. Their silence becomes proclamation.

Remembering God’s promises reshapes how we see our present circumstances.

Today, whatever “tomb” you may be standing near—whether it’s disappointment, fear, or uncertainty—hear the question again: Why are you looking for life here?

Jesus is not confined to what feels dead in your life. He is risen. He is ahead of you. And He is calling you to live in the hope of that truth.

Prayer

Lord, help me to remember Your promises when I feel surrounded by doubt or loss. Teach me to look for life where You are, not where I assume You to be. Renew my hope through the power of the resurrection, and lead me to walk in the new life You have given. Amen.

A New Life of True Freedom

April 5, 2026 • Devotion

⏱ 5 Minute Read

One of the deepest desires of the human heart is to truly know God. In Epistle to the Philippians 3:10, the apostle Paul the Apostle expresses this longing clearly: “That I may know Him, and the power of His resurrection, and the fellowship of His sufferings, being made conformable unto His death.”

Paul’s desire was not simply to know about Christ, but to know Him personally. This kind of knowledge is not intellectual alone—it is transformational. When we truly encounter Christ, our lives begin to change from the inside out.

Jesus speaks of this transformation in Gospel of John 8:36: “If the Son therefore shall make you free, ye shall be free indeed.” Many people think freedom is the ability to follow their own desires, but Scripture reveals that real freedom is found only in Christ. Without Him, we remain bound to sin, guilt, and the patterns of our old nature.

Human reasoning often leads us astray. Book of Proverbs 14:12 warns us: “There is a way which seemeth right unto a man, but the end thereof are the ways of death.” What feels right or appears wise to us may actually lead away from God. That is why we must anchor our lives in God’s truth rather than our own understanding.

(Continue)

Even when people question or resist God’s truth, Scripture reminds us in Epistle to the Romans 3:4: “Let God be true, but every man a liar.” God’s Word stands firm regardless of human opinion. His truth does not change with culture, circumstance, or time.

The good news is that through Christ we are given a completely new identity. Epistle to the Galatians 3:27 says, “For as many of you as have been baptized into Christ have put on Christ.” To put on Christ means we begin to reflect His character, His love, and His righteousness.

This raises an important question that Paul addresses in Epistle to the Romans 6:1: “Shall we continue in sin, that grace may abound?” His answer, of course, is no. Grace is not permission to live as we once did; it is the power to live differently.

When we come to Christ, something remarkable happens. Second Epistle to the Corinthians 5:17 declares: “Therefore if any man be in Christ, he is a new creature: old things are passed away; behold, all things are become new.”

This is the heart of the gospel. In Christ we are not merely improved—we are made new. Our past no longer defines us. Our failures no longer hold us captive. Through Jesus we are given freedom, truth, and a new life.

Today, the invitation is simple: know Christ more deeply, walk in the freedom He provides, and live as the new creation He has made you to be.

Prayer

Lord Jesus, I desire to know You more—the power of Your resurrection and the life You give. Help me walk in the freedom You purchased for me. Guard me from trusting my own understanding, and teach me to live in the truth of Your Word. Thank You for making me a new creation. Let my life reflect You each day. Amen.
