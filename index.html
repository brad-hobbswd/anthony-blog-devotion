Why Do You Seek the Living Among the Dead?

April 6, 2026 • Devotion

At dawn, the women came to the tomb carrying spices, prepared to honor what they believed was the end. Their hopes had been buried alongside Jesus. Grief had settled in. Finality felt certain.

But the stone was rolled away.

Luke tells us that as they stood there, confused and afraid, two men in dazzling clothes spoke words that still echo through history: “Why do you seek the living among the dead? He is not here, but has risen.”

This question gently confronts not just the women—but us.

How often do we look for life in places marked by death? We search for peace in anxiety, purpose in achievement, identity in approval. Like the women at the tomb, we sometimes carry spices to situations God has already transformed, expecting endings where God has declared new beginnings.

The resurrection of Jesus is not just an event to remember; it is a reality to live in. It means that despair does not have the final word. It means that what seems sealed, lost, or broken is not beyond God’s power to restore.

The angels remind the women to remember what Jesus had said, that He would be crucified and rise again. That remembering changes everything. Their sorrow turns into urgency. Their silence becomes proclamation.

Remembering God’s promises reshapes how we see our present circumstances.

Today, whatever “tomb” you may be standing near, whether disappointment, fear, or uncertainty, hear the question again: Why are you looking for life here?

Jesus is not confined to what feels dead in your life. He is risen. He is ahead of you. He is calling you to live in the hope of that truth.

Prayer

Lord Jesus, help me to remember Your promises when I feel surrounded by doubt or loss. Teach me to look for life where You are, not where assumptions lead. Renew my hope through the power of Your resurrection. Lead me to walk in the new life You have given. Amen.

A New Life of True Freedom

April 5, 2026 • Devotion

One of the deepest desires of the human heart is to truly know God.

Paul expresses this longing in Philippians 3:10. He desired not just knowledge about Christ, but a real encounter with Him.

Jesus speaks of true freedom in John 8:36. Freedom is not doing whatever we want. True freedom is found in Him.

Proverbs 14:12 reminds us that what seems right can lead to destruction.

Romans 3:4 declares that God remains true regardless of human opinion.

Galatians 3:27 reveals that we put on Christ and become new.

Romans 6:1 shows that grace leads to transformation, not continued sin.

2 Corinthians 5:17 declares that we are made new.

Prayer

Lord Jesus, help me know You deeply. Teach me to walk in Your truth and live as a new creation. Amen.
