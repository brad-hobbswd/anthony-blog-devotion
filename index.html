Mindful of God’s Business

April 7, 2026 • Devotion

There’s a powerful moment in Scripture when a young Jesus says, “Did you not know that I must be about My Father’s business?” (Luke 2:49, NKJV). Even at a young age, His heart was aligned with God’s purpose. That same calling—to be about God’s business—extends to us today.

But what does it mean to take care of God’s business?

It doesn’t always look like preaching or standing on a stage. Often, it’s found in the quiet, everyday moments: showing kindness when it’s inconvenient, choosing integrity when no one is watching, forgiving when it’s hard, and loving people who may never return it.

(Continue)

God’s business is about reflecting His heart in a world that desperately needs Him.

Jesus also reminds us in Matthew 6:33: But seek first the kingdom of God and His righteousness, and all these things shall be added to you.”

When we prioritize God’s kingdom—His values, His will, His ways—we can trust Him to handle the details of our lives. Taking care of God’s business doesn’t mean neglecting our responsibilities; it means aligning them with His purpose.

Sometimes we get distracted, overwhelmed by our own plans, worries, and ambitions. But Scripture gently redirects us in

Colossians 3:23:
And whatever you do, do it heartily, as to the Lord and not to men.”

Even the smallest task becomes sacred when it’s done for Him.

Reflection

Where has your focus been lately? Are you consumed with your own agenda, or are you making space for God’s priorities?

Prayer

Lord, help me to be about Your business. Align my heart with Yours. Teach me to see every moment as an opportunity to serve You, whether big or small. Give me the wisdom to seek Your kingdom first and the faith to trust You with the rest. Amen.

Why Do You Seek the Living Among the Dead?

April 6, 2026 • Devotion

At dawn, the women came to the tomb carrying spices, prepared to honor what they believed was the end. Their hopes had been buried alongside Jesus. Grief had settled in. Finality felt certain.

But the stone was rolled away.

Luke tells us that as they stood there, confused and afraid, two men in dazzling clothes spoke words that still echo through history: “Why do you seek the living among the dead? He is not here, but has risen.”

This question gently confronts not just the women—but us.

(Continue)

How often do we look for life in places marked by death? We search for peace in anxiety, purpose in achievement, identity in approval. Like the women at the tomb, we sometimes carry spices to situations God has already transformed—expecting endings where God has declared new beginnings.

The resurrection of Jesus is not just an event to remember; it is a reality to live in. It means that despair does not have the final word. It means that what seems sealed, lost, or broken is not beyond God’s power to restore.

The angels remind the women to remember what Jesus had said—that He would be crucified and rise again. And that remembering changes everything. Their sorrow turns into urgency. Their silence becomes proclamation.

Remembering God’s promises reshapes how we see our present circumstances.

Today, whatever “tomb” you may be standing near—whether it’s disappointment, fear, or uncertainty—hear the question again: Why are you looking for life here?

Jesus is not confined to what feels dead in your life. He is risen. He is ahead of you. And He is calling you to live in the hope of that truth.

Prayer

Lord, help me to remember Your promises when I feel surrounded by doubt or loss. Teach me to look for life where You are, not where I assume You to be. Renew my hope through the power of the resurrection, and lead me to walk in the new life You have given. Amen.

A New Life of True Freedom

April 5, 2026 • Devotion

One of the deepest desires of the human heart is to truly know God. In Epistle to the Philippians 3:10, the apostle Paul the Apostle expresses this longing clearly: “That I may know Him, and the power of His resurrection, and the fellowship of His sufferings, being made conformable unto His death.”

Paul’s desire was not simply to know about Christ, but to know Him personally. This kind of knowledge is not intellectual alone—it is transformational. When we truly encounter Christ, our lives begin to change from the inside out.

Jesus speaks of this transformation in Gospel of John 8:36: “If the Son therefore shall make you free, ye shall be free indeed.” Many people think freedom is the ability to follow their own desires, but Scripture reveals that real freedom is found only in Christ. Without Him, we remain bound to sin, guilt, and the patterns of our old nature.

Human reasoning often leads us astray. Book of Proverbs 14:12 warns us: “There is a way which seemeth right unto a man, but the end thereof are the ways of death.” What feels right or appears wise to us may actually lead away from God. That is why we must anchor our lives in God’s truth rather than our own understanding.

(Continue)

Even when people question or resist God’s truth, Scripture reminds us in Epistle to the Romans 3:4: “Let God be true, but every man a liar.” God’s Word stands firm regardless of human opinion. His truth does not change with culture, circumstance, or time.

The good news is that through Christ we are given a completely new identity. Epistle to the Galatians 3:27 says, “For as many of you as have been baptized into Christ have put on Christ.” To put on Christ means we begin to reflect His character, His love, and His righteousness.

This raises an important question that Paul addresses in Epistle to the Romans 6:1: “Shall we continue in sin, that grace may abound?” His answer, of course, is no. Grace is not permission to live as we once did; it is the power to live differently.

When we come to Christ, something remarkable happens. Second Epistle to the Corinthians 5:17 declares: “Therefore if any man be in Christ, he is a new creature: old things are passed away; behold, all things are become new.”

This is the heart of the gospel. In Christ we are not merely improved—we are made new. Our past no longer defines us. Our failures no longer hold us captive. Through Jesus we are given freedom, truth, and a new life.

Today, the invitation is simple: know Christ more deeply, walk in the freedom He provides, and live as the new creation He has made you to be.

Prayer

Lord Jesus, I desire to know You more—the power of Your resurrection and the life You give. Help me walk in the freedom You purchased for me. Guard me from trusting my own understanding, and teach me to live in the truth of Your Word. Thank You for making me a new creation. Let my life reflect You each day. Amen.
